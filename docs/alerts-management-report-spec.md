# Alerts Management Report — specification

Audited from `Alert management presentation 20th July 2026.pptx` (10 slides). The in-app
"Generate Report" feature must reproduce this deck for any selected date range.

The deck has two halves: a **daily** section (slides 1–4, single day) and a **period**
section (slides 5–10, a date range). In the app both halves are driven by one selected
range: when the range is a single day the daily tables use that day; otherwise the same
range drives everything.

## Slide 1 — "Alerts Management report (<date>) · All PHEs" (table)

District-by-district table, grouped by patient status (**Alive** section, then **Dead**
section, each with its own subtotal row), plus a grand **Total** row.

Columns: `District | Signals | Discarded | Field Case Verification | Sample Collected | Pending verification`

- **Signals** = all alerts in range for that district/status.
- **Discarded** = outcome bucket "Discarded".
- **Field Case Verification** = desk-verification action contains Field Case Verification.
- **Sample Collected** = desk-verification action contains Sample Collected.
- **Pending verification** = not yet verified (no desk/field decision).

## Slide 2 — same table filtered to **VHFs** only

Same layout plus an extra **Alerts** column (signals that became alerts, i.e. verified
as true alerts). Filter: disease/PHE classified as VHF.

## Slide 3 — "Signals sources" (pie chart)

Pie of signal counts by source for the daily scope: Community / Health facility /
Point Of Entry (only sources present appear).

## Slide 4 — "Alert details" (narrative table)

Columns: `Source | District | Narrative`. One row per alert in the daily scope.
Narrative is composed as:
`<age> yrs, <M/F>. Symptoms: <symptoms>. History: <case history>. Actions: <actions e.g. Sample Collected, Mortality Surveillance, Validated for EMS>. [Verified by: <name>. Verification date: <date>.]`
Falls back to whatever free-text narrative exists when structured fields are missing.

## Slide 5 — "All PHEs" cascade (grouped bar, series Alive/Dead) — period scope

Categories (in order): `Signals, Signals verified, Alerts, Sample Collected,
Field Case Verification, SDB, RRT deployment, EMS`.

## Slide 6 — same cascade filtered to **VHFs**.

## Slide 7 — "Signal Sources (n=<total>)" (bar) — period scope

Counts by source: Health facility, Community, 6767, eCHIS, Other, Refugee Camp,
Direct call, POE, Schools (descending).

## Slide 8 — "Other PHEs reported: Alerts" (bar)

Alert counts by disease/PHE (VHFs bucket first, then Routine, Malaria,
Measles/Rubella, Anthrax, … descending).

## Slide 9 — Map + "Top 10 district registering alerts"

- Left: Uganda district choropleth of alert counts for the period, binned legend
  (No alerts / quartile-ish bins), title "Map showing distribution of alerts, N=<n>".
  Generated by capturing the in-app alerts map as PNG.
- Right: horizontal/vertical bar of top ~10 districts, stacked/grouped series
  `VHFs` vs `Other PHEs`.

## Slide 10 — "Trend of signals vs alerts reported (<month start> to date)"

Daily line chart, series **Signals** and **Alerts**, from a trend start (deck used
1 June 2026) through the end of the selected range. Signals = all records/day,
Alerts = records escalated to alerts/day.

## Notes

- Slide notes explain: "About the PHEs, VHF are 5, the rest were recorded as Other
  or Routine" — VHF classification is a fixed disease list.
- Title date formats: single day `20th July 2026`; range `13th–19th July 2026`
  (ordinal day + month + year).
